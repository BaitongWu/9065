Homelessness is a significant problem in Canada. Most cities and many smaller communities operate shelters and outreach services that focus on providing assistance to people who are homeless, so there are many ways you can make a difference. The Salvation Army operates homeless shelters and soup kitchens in many communities. Enter your zip code on their website to find a shelter near you, then reach out and ask how you can help. The National Coalition for the Homeless publishes a directory of shelters and other resources on their website, so that is a good place to search for local programs that focus on helping people who are homeless.

Not having a place to live significantly impacts a person's mental and physical health as well as communities at large. By working to end it, we can connect homeless individuals and families not only with permanent, affordable housing, but also with stable jobs and quality education. And while there are many ways you can help the homeless, from volunteering to simply donating old clothing or handing out food, choosing to dedicate your career to the cause will have the greatest impact. Remember that homelessness is a complex issue with many contributing factors, so long-term solutions involve a combination of support, policy changes, and community engagement. By taking even small steps to help the homeless, you can make a meaningful impact on their lives and contribute to broader efforts to address this issue.
